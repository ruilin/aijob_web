      The method

      Jobs are bundles of tasks.

      AI doesn't replace jobs; it absorbs tasks. Every role is a mix of things — some routine and digital, some physical, human, or high-stakes. The Exposure Index estimates what share of a role's typical tasks current and near-term AI can already do well, then lets you adjust for how your own day actually breaks down.

      Scores lean on the pattern found across task-level automation research: language, data, and predictable digital work sits closest to what today's models are good at, while physical dexterity, real human trust, and accountable judgment stay hardest to automate.

      Common questions

      Will AI take your job? — the honest FAQ

      Will AI take my job?
      The honest answer is rarely a simple yes or no. AI comes for tasks, not titles — so the real question is how much of your day it can already do. The AI Exposure Index gives a task-level read: for your role it estimates what share of typical tasks current and near-term AI can meaningfully do, then lets you adjust for how your own work actually breaks down.
      What is the AI Exposure Index?
      It's a directional 0–100 score estimating how much of a role's typical tasks current and near-term AI can already do well. A low score means AI mostly assists you; a high score means the work — and how many people do it — is likely to change a lot. It measures how much the work changes, not whether the job disappears.
      How is AI exposure calculated?
      Every role is a bundle of tasks. The index estimates what share of a role's typical tasks today's AI can do, informed by task-level automation research: routine, text, code and data work sits closest to what models are good at, while physical dexterity, in-person trust, and accountable judgment stay hardest to automate. Six questions then fine-tune the score to your actual day.
      Which jobs are most exposed to AI?
      Roles built on routine, digital, language and data work tend to score highest — for example data entry clerks, telemarketers, bookkeepers, translators, copywriters and customer support. Roles built on hands-on physical skill, in-person trust and high-stakes human judgment — such as nurses, electricians, plumbers and firefighters — tend to score lowest.
      Is the AI Exposure Index free and private?
      Yes. It's completely free with no account required. Everything runs in your browser — nothing you enter is sent to a server, tracked, or stored anywhere.
